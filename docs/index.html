Pfizer, Arvinas score 1st PROTAC nod with Veppanu in breast cancer

Fierce Pharma · 2026-05-05 · ⏱ 3 min read

The FDA approved Veppanu (vepdegestrant), the first-ever PROTAC (protein degrader) therapy, for ER-positive breast cancer patients with ESR1 mutations, showing 43% reduction in disease progression risk versus standard care. Despite this milestone, Pfizer and Arvinas are seeking a third-party partner to commercialize the drug following mixed phase 3 results that limited its market potential.

💡 This validates an entirely new drug modality that degrades proteins rather than just blocking them, potentially opening therapeutic pathways across oncology and neurological diseases while establishing proof-of-concept for the broader PROTAC platform.
