OpenAI's o1 correctly diagnosed 67% of ER patients vs. 50-55% by triage doctors

Harvard Medical School · 2026-05-04 · ⏱ 5 min read

A Harvard study published in Science found that OpenAI's o1 model outperformed physicians in emergency room diagnostic accuracy across multiple tests, correctly diagnosing 67% of triage cases versus 50-55% for doctors. The AI excelled particularly in rare disease diagnosis and complex medical reasoning tasks, using real patient data from Beth Israel and Mass General hospitals. Researchers emphasize this suggests AI-physician partnership rather than replacement, with urgent need for prospective clinical trials.

💡 This represents a landmark validation of AI diagnostic capabilities in real clinical settings, potentially accelerating adoption of LLMs for clinical decision support and reshaping how medical diagnosis is approached in emergency departments.
