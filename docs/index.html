Aldeyra Therapeutics' Dry Eye Drug Suffers Third FDA Rejection, Shares Plummet 75%

BioSpace · 2026-03-18

The FDA rejected Aldeyra's lead drug candidate reproxalap for the third time, citing "a lack of substantial evidence" and failure "to demonstrate efficacy in adequate and well controlled studies" for dry eye disease treatment. The company's stock crashed over 70% to $1.25 per share following the rejection. The FDA did not recommend additional trials or request more confirmatory evidence.

💡 CEO Todd Brady emphasized the company has no plans to give up on reproxalap, calling it "the only drug with clinical activity within minutes of administration" for dry eye patients, but the third rejection raises serious questions about the drug's future and Aldeyra's pipeline prospects.

Eli Lilly Commits $3 Billion to Build Obesity Pill Manufacturing in China

Bloomberg/BioSpace · 2026-03-18

Eli Lilly will invest $3 billion over 10 years in China operations to prepare local production of its oral obesity drug orforglipron, partnering with companies like Pharmaron Beijing Pharmaceutical. The investment will establish local production of oral solid medications, particularly orforglipron, which is currently under regulatory review globally. The money will boost drug supply in China while contributing to employment and local biotech development.

💡 The massive investment signals Lilly's confidence in oral GLP-1 drugs and China's obesity market potential, while reducing supply chain risks and tariff exposure as the company races against Novo Nordisk for global obesity market dominance.

Mistral AI Launches Forge Platform to Challenge OpenAI in Enterprise Custom Model Training

TechCrunch · 2026-03-18

Mistral announced Mistral Forge at Nvidia GTC, a platform that lets enterprises build custom models trained from scratch on their own data rather than fine-tuning existing models. CEO Arthur Mensch says the company is on track to surpass $1 billion in annual recurring revenue this year. Unlike competitors that focus on fine-tuning, Mistral enables training models from scratch, potentially offering better handling of non-English or domain-specific data and greater control over model behavior.

💡 This positions Mistral as a direct competitor to OpenAI and Anthropic in the enterprise market, targeting companies that need "correct, secure, and repeatable" AI rather than general-purpose models.

🤖 Slot 3

OpenAI Releases GPT-5.4 Mini and Nano Models for High-Volume, Low-Latency Applications

OpenAI · 2026-03-18

OpenAI released GPT-5.4 mini and nano, designed for high-volume workloads, with the mini version running more than 2x faster than GPT-5 mini while significantly improving across coding, reasoning, multimodal understanding, and tool use. GPT-5.4 nano is the smallest, cheapest version optimized for classification, data extraction, ranking, and coding subagents that handle simpler supporting tasks. The models target scenarios where "the best model is often not the largest one—it's the one that can respond quickly, use tools reliably, and still perform well on complex professional tasks."

💡 This represents a strategic shift toward specialized, efficient AI models for enterprise applications, enabling cost-effective scaling of AI workflows where speed matters more than maximum capability, directly competing with similar offerings from Anthropic and emerging players.
