Regeneron wins landmark FDA approval for first gene therapy treating hearing loss

FDA / Multiple · 2026-04-30 · ⏱ 3 min read

The FDA approved Regeneron's Otarmeni, the first-ever gene therapy for genetic hearing loss, using a dual AAV vector to treat children and adults with OTOF gene mutations. The approval came through an accelerated 61-day review under the National Priority Voucher program, with clinical trial data showing 80% of patients experienced improved hearing. This marks the first disease-modifying treatment for OTOF-related deafness, which affects 2-8% of inherited hearing loss cases.

💡 This landmark approval validates Regeneron's gene therapy platform in a new therapeutic area and could signal the beginning of a broader sensory restoration market, with potential applications beyond hearing loss.

Getting NIH funds is more competitive — and confusing — than ever

STAT News · 2026-04-30 · ⏱ 6 min read

NIH grant success rates have plummeted to just 13% under new budgeting strategies and policy changes, forcing academic researchers to spend more time on grant applications while many labs face funding crises. A STAT survey of nearly 1,000 researchers found 45% experienced grant delays and 43% cancelled research projects, with the agency spending 30% less than normal at this point in the fiscal year.

💡 Severe NIH funding constraints could reshape the biomedical innovation landscape, potentially driving more academic talent toward industry partnerships and affecting the pipeline of early-stage research that feeds into drug development.
