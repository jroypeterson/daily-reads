FDA Approves First Non-Antipsychotic Drug to Treat Agitation Associated with Dementia

FDA Press Releases · 2026-05-01 · ⏱ 3 min read

The FDA expanded approval for Auvelity (dextromethorphan/bupropion) to treat agitation in Alzheimer's dementia patients, making it the first non-antipsychotic option for this indication. The approval was based on two randomized trials showing efficacy and received breakthrough therapy designation. Auvelity was originally approved in 2022 for major depressive disorder.

💡 This creates a new therapeutic category addressing a major unmet need in dementia care, potentially opening significant market opportunities for Axsome Therapeutics given the aging population and limitations of current antipsychotic treatments.
