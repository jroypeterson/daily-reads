FDA approves first-ever PROTAC drug Veppanu from Arvinas and Pfizer for breast cancer

BioPharma Dive / Endpoints News · 2026-05-02 · ⏱ 3 min read

The FDA approved Arvinas and Pfizer's Veppanu, the first-ever PROTAC (protein degradation) drug to reach market, for treating ESR1-mutated breast cancer patients. Despite the regulatory milestone validating a new drug class, the companies are seeking a third-party partner due to competitive market dynamics and uncertain commercial prospects against existing oral hormone-degrading drugs from Lilly and Menarini.

💡 This approval validates protein degradation as a viable therapeutic approach beyond traditional drug mechanisms, potentially opening new treatment paradigms across oncology and neurological diseases, while Arvinas' 7% stock jump reflects immediate investor recognition of this scientific breakthrough.

AI startup reaches ChatGPT moment: Eka's robotic claw breakthrough suggests hardware inflection point

Hacker News / Wired · 2026-05-02 · ⏱ 6 min read

Eka Robotics has developed robotic manipulation technology that demonstrates unprecedented dexterity, able to naturally handle delicate tasks like screwing in light bulbs that have stumped other commercial robot arms. The startup's approach builds on lessons from OpenAI's abandoned Dactyl project, using improved simulation-to-reality techniques to achieve fluid, natural-seeming robotic movement that could unlock broader automation applications beyond current factory/warehouse use cases.
