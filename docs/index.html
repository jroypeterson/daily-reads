FDA Approves First-Ever PROTAC Drug Veppanu for Breast Cancer

Fierce Biotech · 2026-05-03 · ⏱ 3 min read

The FDA approved Veppanu (vepdegestrant) from Pfizer and Arvinas, marking the first-ever approval of a PROTAC protein degrader therapy for ESR1-mutated breast cancer. The drug reduced disease progression risk by 43% versus standard care in phase 3 trials, though it failed in the broader unselected population. The companies are seeking a third-party partner for commercialization following the mixed clinical results.

💡 This breakthrough validates an entirely new therapeutic class (protein degraders) that could transform drug development across oncology and other diseases, while demonstrating how biotech innovation translates to commercial reality.
