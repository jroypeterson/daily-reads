CERN uses tiny AI models burned into silicon for real-time LHC data filtering

The Open Reader · 2026-03-28

CERN is using extremely small AI models physically embedded in silicon chips to perform real-time filtering of the massive data streams from the Large Hadron Collider. These tiny models process hundreds of terabytes per second and make decisions in nanoseconds, using FPGAs and ASICs rather than traditional GPUs.

💡 This represents a compelling counter-trend to the industry's focus on ever-larger AI models, demonstrating how specialized 'tiny AI' can achieve extreme performance in scientific applications.

Arm releases first in-house chip, with Meta as debut customer

CNBC · 2026-03-28

After 35+ years of only licensing chip designs, Arm unveiled its first physical silicon product - the AGI CPU for AI data centers. 💡 Arm's entry into physical silicon manufacturing creates a new competitive dynamic in the AI chip market and signals the growing importance of CPU infrastructure for agentic AI workloads.

🌀 Slot 4

Go hard on agents, not on your filesystem

Stanford · 2026-03-28

Stanford researchers released 'jai', a lightweight Linux sandbox tool for safely running AI agents without giving them full system access. It addresses growing reports of AI tools causing filesystem damage by providing effortless containment with copy-on-write overlays for home directories.

💡 As AI agents become more autonomous and capable, security tooling like this becomes critical infrastructure for safe deployment - a practical solution to an emerging enterprise risk.
