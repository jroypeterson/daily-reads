J&J, Protagonist's Groundbreaking Psoriasis Pill Icotyde Wins FDA Approval

BioSpace / STAT News / FiercePharma · 2026-03-19

Johnson & Johnson won U.S. approval for a daily psoriasis pill that rivals the benefits of injectable medicines and could roil a multibillion-dollar market, with the company expecting the drug to bring in more than $5 billion in annual sales at its peak. The FDA approved Icotyde (icotrokinra) for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis patients 12 and older, marking a "potential game-changer" that aligns with new guidance recommending patients move to systemic therapy after topical treatments. The approval marks Protagonist Therapeutics' transition to commercial stage with a $50 million milestone payment, with analysts seeing potential for Icotyde to differentiate and capture new patient segments from injectable options.

💡 First-in-class oral IL-23 receptor antagonist could reshape the $10+ billion psoriasis market dominated by injectables, with major implications for J&J's immunology portfolio and Protagonist's commercial prospects.

J&J Reigns Supreme as Top Pharma by Revenue While Lilly Surges on Obesity Boom

BioSpace · 2026-03-19

🤖 Slot 3

ICML Conference Desk-Rejects 2% of Papers After Watermarking Catches LLM Review Violations

ICML Blog · 2026-03-19

ICML desk-rejected 497 papers (~2% of submissions) after detecting that 506 reviewers who agreed to Policy A (no LLM use) violated rules by using LLMs in their reviews, caught through innovative watermarking techniques. The detection involved watermarking submission PDFs with hidden LLM instructions that would influence any LLM-generated review, with success rates over 80% for most frontier models. False positive rates were strongly controlled with every flagged instance manually inspected to ensure reviews weren't simply mentioning the watermark was present.

💡 Demonstrates escalating tensions around AI use in academic peer review, with conferences implementing sophisticated detection methods that could influence how AI tools are integrated into research workflows.
