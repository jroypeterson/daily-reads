Merck to Buy Terns Pharmaceuticals for $6.7 Billion Ahead of Keytruda Patent Cliff

CNBC/BioSpace · 2026-03-26

Merck will acquire Terns for $53 per share in cash, amounting to an equity value of about $6.7 billion, marking the third multibillion-dollar acquisition for Merck over the past year as it prepares for its best-selling cancer drug Keytruda losing key patent protection in 2028. Terns is developing TERN-701, a novel investigational oral allosteric BCR::ABL1 tyrosine kinase inhibitor for chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) that analysts see as a multibillion-dollar drug with potential to rival Novartis' Scemblix.

💡 This acquisition reflects pharma's aggressive M&A strategy to offset patent cliff losses, with Keytruda generating nearly $32B annually and representing half of Merck's sales.

📊 Slot 2

Pharma R&D Spending Drops 3.6% as Companies Restructure Pipelines

BioSpace · 2026-03-26

R&D spending at the top 16 pharmaceutical companies declined by 3.6% overall in 2025 to $159.1 billion, with Johnson & Johnson's research expenses falling by nearly 15% to $14.7 billion. Bristol Myers Squibb dropped R&D spending by 11% to $9.95 billion, Merck cut R&D expenses by 12% due to lower business development charges, and Pfizer's R&D fell 4% to $10.21 billion amid company-wide restructuring.

💡 This industry-wide R&D pullback signals a strategic shift toward M&A and licensing over internal innovation, with implications for biotech funding and drug development timelines.

🤖 Slot 3

ARC-AGI-3 Benchmark Exposes Critical Gap in AI's Path to General Intelligence

Fast Company/ARC Prize · 2026-03-26

The ARC Prize Foundation released ARC-AGI-3, a new benchmark that may offer the clearest measurement yet of how close today's AI agents are to human-level intelligence, consisting of more than a thousand simple, video-game-like scenarios designed to measure on-the-fly reasoning rather than memory recall. As in previous ARC benchmarks, humans can navigate the tasks with relative ease, while current AI agents score zero points, failing even the developer preview of just three games.

💡 This benchmark reveals fundamental limitations in current AI systems' reasoning capabilities, potentially impacting AI investment strategies and expectations for AGI timelines across tech companies.
