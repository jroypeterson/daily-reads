Tim Cook to Step Down as Apple CEO; John Ternus to Succeed

Multiple sources · 2026-04-29 · ⏱ 3 min read

Tim Cook will step down as Apple CEO on September 1, 2026, transitioning to executive chairman while John Ternus, current SVP of Hardware Engineering, becomes the new CEO. Under Cook's 15-year tenure, Apple grew from $350B to $4T market cap with revenue nearly quadrupling to $416B, while expanding globally and launching major new categories like Apple Watch and Vision Pro. Ternus, a 25-year Apple veteran who worked under both Steve Jobs and Cook, will join the board as he takes over leadership.

💡 This represents a pivotal leadership transition at the world's most valuable company, with Ternus's hardware engineering background potentially signaling Apple's strategic direction as it faces AI competition and seeks post-iPhone growth drivers.
